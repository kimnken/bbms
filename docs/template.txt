CHAPTER ONE
INTRODUCTION
1.1 Background of the Study
Effective communication is a fundamental component of any academic institution because it ensures that important information is delivered to students and staff in a timely and efficient manner. Universities frequently disseminate announcements related to academic schedules, examination timetables, project deadlines, campus events, and administrative notices. Traditionally, these announcements are communicated through institutional websites, notice boards, emails, and social media platforms. However, many students do not consistently access these communication channels, which often results in delayed awareness or complete lack of awareness of important academic information. Information systems play a significant role in improving communication within organizations by enabling the efficient collection, processing, and dissemination of information (Laudon & Laudon, 2022).
With the rapid advancement of digital technologies, universities are increasingly adopting intelligent systems to improve information management and communication processes. Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as one of the most transformative technologies capable of enhancing the performance of modern information systems. AI enables computer systems to analyze large volumes of data, recognize patterns, and make intelligent decisions that assist users in accessing relevant information. According to Russell and Norvig (2021), Artificial Intelligence refers to the study and design of computer systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem solving. These capabilities make AI particularly useful for developing intelligent information systems that can automatically analyze and prioritize large volumes of information. One of the most widely used applications of Artificial Intelligence in modern digital systems is the development of recommendation systems. Recommendation systems are designed to help users identify relevant information by filtering and prioritizing content based on user preferences, characteristics, or behavior. Ricci et al. (2022) explain that recommendation systems are widely used in various domains, including e-commerce, online learning platforms, and digital media services, to deliver personalized content to users. Similarly, Raza et al. (2024) note that recommendation technologies help reduce information overload by presenting users with the most relevant information from large datasets.
In the context of university environments, the integration of AI-driven recommendation systems can significantly improve the way campus announcements are delivered to students. Most existing announcement systems display information chronologically without considering the relevance of the information to specific students. As a result, students may struggle to identify the most important announcements among a large volume of information. By applying AI-based recommendation techniques, announcements can be intelligently filtered and prioritized according to factors such as student department, academic level, and category of information. This approach can significantly improve the accessibility and relevance of campus information while reducing the need for students to constantly check institutional websites or social media platforms. Therefore, this study proposes the development of an AI-Based Campus Announcement Recommendation and Prioritization System designed to improve the dissemination of campus announcements in universities. The proposed system will apply Artificial Intelligence techniques to analyze announcements and deliver relevant information directly to students in a structured and prioritized manner. By integrating intelligent recommendation mechanisms, the system aims to improve communication efficiency and ensure that students receive timely and relevant academic information.
1.2 Problem Statement
One of the major challenges faced by students in many universities is the difficulty in accessing important campus announcements in a timely manner. Most institutions rely heavily on websites, social media platforms, and physical notice boards to disseminate information. However, students may not regularly visit these platforms, which often results in missed announcements regarding lectures, deadlines, or important academic updates. This lack of direct and centralized access to announcements reduces the effectiveness of communication between the institution and students.
Another major limitation of the existing announcement systems is the absence of intelligent filtering and prioritization of information. Most current systems simply display announcements in chronological order without considering their relevance to specific students. As a result, students are often overwhelmed with large amounts of information, including announcements that may not be directly related to their department, level, or academic activities. This makes it difficult for students to quickly identify the most important announcements that require their attention.
Furthermore, existing systems lack the ability to intelligently recommend announcements based on user needs and behavior. The integration of an Artificial Intelligence model in the proposed system will help address this limitation by analyzing announcement content and prioritizing relevant information for individual users. By applying AI techniques such as intelligent filtering and recommendation algorithms, the proposed system aims to improve the efficiency of campus communication and ensure that students receive important announcements without depending on external platforms such as websites or social media.

2.2 Conceptual Review
2.2.1 Digital Announcement Systems
Digital announcement systems have become an essential component of communication infrastructure in educational institutions. These systems are designed to facilitate the dissemination of information such as academic schedules, examination updates, event notifications, and emergency alerts. Traditionally, institutions relied on physical notice boards, which were limited by accessibility and timeliness. With the advancement of technology, these systems have evolved into web-based and mobile-based platforms.
Web-based announcement systems allow administrators to post updates that can be accessed through institutional portals. While these systems provide centralized access to information, they require users to actively log in to retrieve updates. This dependency on user initiative significantly reduces the effectiveness of such systems, particularly in situations where immediate awareness is required (Johnson et al., 2020).
Mobile-based systems, on the other hand, leverage push notifications to deliver updates directly to users. Although this approach improves immediacy, it introduces new challenges such as device dependency, notification fatigue, and inconsistent internet connectivity. Studies have shown that users often ignore or disable notifications due to excessive alerts, thereby reducing the reliability of mobile-based communication systems (Adebayo & Yusuf, 2020). In response to these limitations, digital signage systems have been introduced as an alternative means of information dissemination. These systems utilize display screens placed in strategic locations to broadcast announcements to a wider audience. While digital signage improves visibility and accessibility, most implementations lack intelligent content management, resulting in the display of irrelevant or redundant information (Smith & Lee, 2022). The limitations of existing digital announcement systems highlight the need for more advanced solutions that combine accessibility with intelligent content delivery. This study addresses this need by proposing a system that integrates artificial intelligence and contextual awareness to enhance the effectiveness of announcement dissemination.
2.2.2 Artificial Intelligence in Information Systems
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as a transformative technology in the field of information systems, enabling automation, intelligent decision-making, and data-driven insights. AI techniques, particularly machine learning, have been widely applied in areas such as data analysis, pattern recognition, and predictive modeling.
In the context of information dissemination, AI can be used to analyze the content of announcements and determine their relevance based on predefined criteria. For example, machine learning algorithms can classify announcements into categories such as academic, administrative, or social events. Additionally, AI can assign priority levels to announcements, ensuring that critical information is displayed more prominently (Chen et al., 2019).
One of the key advantages of AI is its ability to learn from data and improve performance over time. By analyzing historical data, AI systems can identify patterns and optimize decision-making processes. However, many existing AI-driven systems rely heavily on user-specific data, which raises concerns regarding privacy and data security.
Furthermore, AI systems often require significant computational resources, which may limit their scalability in certain environments. Despite these challenges, the integration of AI into announcement systems offers significant potential for improving efficiency and relevance. This study leverages AI techniques to develop a system that intelligently selects and prioritizes announcements based on contextual factors rather than personal user data.
2.2.3 Recommendation Systems
Recommendation systems are a class of information filtering systems designed to predict and present relevant content to users. These systems have become increasingly important in the digital age, where users are often overwhelmed by large volumes of information.
There are several types of recommendation systems, including content-based filtering, collaborative filtering, and hybrid approaches. Content-based filtering relies on the characteristics of items to make recommendations, while collaborative filtering uses user behavior and preferences to identify patterns (Kumar & Patel, 2021). In academic environments, recommendation systems have been used to suggest learning materials, courses, and notifications. However, most implementations are designed for personalized recommendations, which may not be suitable for scenarios where information is intended for a broad audience.
Additionally, traditional recommendation systems depend on user interaction and historical data, which may not always be available or reliable. This limitation is particularly relevant in the context of announcement systems, where real-time relevance is more important than historical preferences. The proposed system adopts a modified approach to recommendation by focusing on contextual factors such as location, time, and audience type. This approach eliminates the need for user-specific data while ensuring that relevant information is delivered effectively.

2.2.4 Context-Aware Computing
Context-aware computing is a paradigm that enables systems to adapt their behavior based on environmental conditions. This approach is particularly relevant in scenarios where user interaction is limited or unavailable.
Context-aware systems utilize various data sources, including sensors and system inputs, to gather information about the environment. This information is then used to make decisions and adjust system behavior accordingly. For example, a context-aware system can display different announcements depending on the time of day or the location of the display screen (Wang et al., 2018).
The integration of context-aware computing into announcement systems offers several advantages. It reduces the need for user interaction, enhances the relevance of displayed information, and minimizes information overload. By considering factors such as location, time, and audience type, context-aware systems can deliver targeted and meaningful content. This study leverages context-aware computing to develop a system that dynamically selects and displays announcements based on environmental conditions. This approach ensures that users receive the most relevant information without requiring active participation.
2.2.5 Smart Campus Systems
The concept of a smart campus involves the integration of advanced technologies to create an intelligent and interconnected environment. Smart campus systems utilize technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT), artificial intelligence, and cloud computing to enhance various aspects of campus life, including communication, security, and resource management.
In the context of information dissemination, smart campus systems aim to provide real-time updates through multiple channels. However, many existing implementations still rely on traditional communication methods, limiting their effectiveness (Garcia et al., 2022). The proposed system aligns with the smart campus vision by integrating AI and contextual awareness to improve announcement delivery. By leveraging smart display screens and intelligent algorithms, the system enhances communication efficiency and ensures that relevant information is delivered to the appropriate audience.

2.7 Taxonomy of Intelligent Announcement Systems
Before developing an intelligent announcement display system, it is important to understand how existing systems can be classified based on their operational characteristics, level of intelligence, and mode of information delivery. A taxonomy provides a structured framework for organizing these systems into meaningful categories, thereby enabling a clearer understanding of their evolution and limitations.
The classification of announcement systems can be broadly divided into three major categories: traditional systems, digital systems, and intelligent systems. Traditional systems include manual methods such as physical notice boards, which are limited by accessibility and timeliness. Digital systems improve upon this by utilizing web and mobile technologies, but they still depend heavily on user interaction. Intelligent systems represent the most advanced category, incorporating artificial intelligence and context-aware computing to automate decision-making and improve relevance. Furthermore, intelligent systems can be subdivided based on their underlying technologies. These include AI-based systems, recommendation systems, and context-aware systems. AI-based systems focus on classification and prediction, recommendation systems emphasize content filtering, while context-aware systems adapt dynamically to environmental factors such as location and time. The taxonomy also considers the mode of delivery, which includes device-dependent systems (such as mobile applications) and device-independent systems (such as smart display screens). This distinction is particularly important in the context of this study, as the proposed system eliminates reliance on personal devices.
Fig 2.7 illustrates the hierarchical classification of announcement systems, starting from basic traditional methods to advanced intelligent systems. At the foundational level, traditional systems such as notice boards rely entirely on manual processes. These systems are limited in their ability to provide timely updates and are often inaccessible to a large portion of the target audience. Digital systems represent a significant improvement by leveraging web technologies, mobile applications, and SMS-based communication. While these systems enhance accessibility, they still depend on user devices and require active participation from users. This dependency creates a gap in real-time information delivery, as users may not always check their devices or access institutional platforms. Intelligent systems address these limitations by incorporating artificial intelligence and context-aware computing. AI-based systems enable the classification and prioritization of announcements, ensuring that important information is highlighted. Recommendation systems further refine this process by filtering content based on relevance, although traditional implementations often rely on user-specific data. Context-aware systems provide an additional layer of intelligence by adapting to environmental conditions. For example, announcements displayed in a lecture hall may differ from those shown in a cafeteria, depending on the audience and time of day. This dynamic adaptability significantly enhances the effectiveness of information dissemination.
Finally, the taxonomy highlights the distinction between device-dependent and device-independent delivery methods. Device-dependent systems require users to access information through personal devices, while device-independent systems deliver information through shared infrastructure such as smart screens. The proposed system falls into the latter category, making it more inclusive and efficient.

3.2 Analysis of the Existing System 
The existing campus announcement systems are primarily based on traditional communication channels such as notice boards, institutional websites, emails, and social media platforms. While these systems are widely used, they exhibit several limitations that hinder effective communication within academic environments. One of the major limitations is the reliance on active user participation. Students and staff are required to manually check different platforms to access announcements. This approach is inefficient, as users may forget or choose not to check these platforms regularly. Consequently, important announcements such as examination schedules, deadlines, and urgent notices may be missed.
Another significant limitation is the lack of intelligent filtering and prioritization mechanisms. In most existing systems, announcements are presented in a uniform manner without considering their urgency, relevance, or target audience. This leads to information overload, where users are exposed to excessive and often irrelevant information. As a result, critical announcements may be overlooked. Additionally, existing systems are heavily dependent on personal devices such as smartphones and laptops. This creates accessibility challenges, as not all users may have constant access to these devices. Furthermore, relying on personal devices contradicts the goal of providing universal and immediate access to information within the campus. The use of social media platforms for announcements also introduces distractions and lack of structure. Academic announcements are often mixed with non-academic content, making it difficult for users to focus on important information. This reduces the effectiveness of communication and may lead to delays in accessing critical updates. Moreover, existing systems lack context awareness, meaning they do not adapt the information displayed based on location, time, or audience. This results in inefficient communication, as users may receive announcements that are not relevant to them. These limitations highlight the need for a more intelligent and efficient system that can automatically deliver relevant announcements to users without requiring active participation.

3.3 Proposed System Design 
The proposed system is an AI-Based Campus Announcement Recommendation and Prioritization System designed to improve the delivery of information within a campus environment. The system eliminates the need for users to access announcements through personal devices by utilizing smart screens strategically placed across various locations.
The system is designed as a full-stack application consisting of multiple interconnected modules that work together to process, analyze, and display announcements. These modules include the announcement processing module, AI classification module, context-aware recommendation module, and smart screen display module. The announcement processing module allows administrators to input announcements into the system. These announcements are stored in a structured database along with metadata such as category, priority level, target audience, location, and expiration date. The AI classification module processes the announcements and assigns appropriate categories and priority levels. This module uses machine learning techniques to analyze the content of announcements and identify patterns that determine their classification. The context-aware recommendation module is the core component of the system. It evaluates contextual factors such as the location of the smart screen, the time of day, the target audience, and the priority level of announcements. Based on these factors, the system selects the most relevant announcements to display. The smart screen display module serves as the frontend interface of the system. It receives processed data from the backend in the form of structured data (e.g., JSON) and displays it in a visually organized format. The interface is designed to be clear, readable, and easy to understand, ensuring that users can quickly access important information. The system ensures that each smart screen displays announcements that are relevant to its specific environment. For example, screens located in academic buildings display academic-related announcements, while those in administrative areas display general information.

The system architecture defines the overall structure of the proposed system and the interaction between its components. 3.8.3 Sequence Diagram
Fig 3.8.3 illustrates the dynamic behavior of the system by showing the interaction between different components over time. It provides a step-by-step representation of how an announcement is processed from input to output.
The process begins when the Admin submits an announcement through the interface. This request is received by the Backend, which validates the input and ensures that all required fields are provided. The backend then stores the announcement in the Database, ensuring that it is available for future retrieval and processing. After storing the data, the backend forwards the announcement to the AI Model for classification. The AI Model analyzes the textual content of the announcement and assigns a category and priority level. This information is then returned to the backend, which updates the announcement record accordingly.
Next, the backend sends the classified announcement to the Context Engine, which evaluates contextual factors such as location, time, and audience. Based on this analysis, the engine filters and selects the most relevant announcements. The filtered data is then sent back to the backend, which prepares a structured JSON response and transmits it to the Smart Screen. The smart screen receives the data and displays the announcements to users. This sequence ensures efficient data processing and real-time information delivery, demonstrating how different system components collaborate to achieve the desired functionality.

 

3.9 Data Collection and Preprocessing
The effectiveness of any intelligent system largely depends on the quality and structure of the data used during its development. In this study, the data utilized consists of textual campus announcements collected from multiple institutional sources. These sources include departmental notice boards, faculty communications, administrative offices, and existing digital platforms such as school portals and mailing systems. The dataset is composed of various types of announcements, including academic schedules, examination timetables, event notifications, deadlines, and emergency alerts. Collecting data from diverse sources ensures that the system captures a wide range of announcement patterns and improves its generalization capability.
To ensure the reliability and usability of the dataset, a structured data collection approach is adopted. Announcements are gathered over a specified period and categorized based on their purpose and urgency. This categorization helps in labeling the dataset for supervised learning, which is essential for training classification models. According to Tom M. Mitchell (1997), the performance of a machine learning model is directly influenced by the quality of the training data, making proper data collection a critical step in system development.
Following data collection, preprocessing is performed to transform raw textual data into a structured format suitable for machine learning algorithms. Raw text data often contains noise, inconsistencies, and irrelevant information that can negatively impact model performance. Therefore, preprocessing plays a vital role in improving the accuracy and efficiency of the AI model.
The first step in preprocessing is text cleaning, which involves removing unwanted characters such as punctuation marks, special symbols, and HTML tags. This step ensures that only meaningful textual content is retained. Next, text normalization is applied, where all text is converted to lowercase to maintain uniformity and reduce redundancy in word representation. Another important preprocessing step is tokenization, which involves breaking down sentences into individual words or tokens. Tokenization enables the model to analyze text at a granular level. In addition, stop-word removal is performed to eliminate commonly used words such as “the”, “is”, and “and” that do not contribute significantly to the meaning of the text. Removing these words reduces computational complexity and enhances model performance. Furthermore, feature extraction techniques such as Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF) are applied to convert textual data into numerical representations that can be processed by machine learning algorithms. According to Christopher D. Manning et al. (2008), feature extraction is essential in text classification tasks as it helps in identifying the most informative terms within a dataset.
In summary, the data collection and preprocessing stages ensure that the dataset is clean, structured, and suitable for training the AI model. These steps significantly improve the system’s ability to accurately classify and prioritize announcements, thereby enhancing the overall effectiveness of the proposed system.
 3.10 Model Design and Training
The model design phase focuses on developing an intelligent system capable of automatically classifying announcements and assigning appropriate priority levels. The model is designed as a supervised machine learning classifier that processes textual input and produces structured outputs in the form of categories and priority levels. The primary objective of the model is to analyze the content of announcements and determine their relevance based on predefined categories such as academic, event, general, and emergency. In addition, the model assigns priority levels (high, medium, low) based on the urgency of the information. This dual-output design enhances the system’s ability to organize and present announcements effectively. The model is trained using labeled datasets obtained during the data collection phase. Each announcement in the dataset is tagged with its corresponding category and priority level. This labeled data enables the model to learn patterns and relationships between textual features and classification outputs.
Several machine learning algorithms are considered for the classification task, including Naïve Bayes, Logistic Regression, and Support Vector Machines (SVM). Naïve Bayes is particularly suitable for text classification due to its simplicity and efficiency, while Logistic Regression provides probabilistic outputs that are useful for decision-making. Support Vector Machines, on the other hand, are effective in handling high-dimensional data and achieving high classification accuracy. The training process involves splitting the dataset into training and testing sets. The training set is used to teach the model, while the testing set is used to evaluate its performance. Evaluation metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score are used to measure the effectiveness of the model. According to Ian Goodfellow et al. (2016), proper model evaluation is essential to ensure that the system generalizes well to unseen data. In addition to classification, the model incorporates a priority assignment mechanism that evaluates keywords such as “urgent”, “deadline”, and “exam” to determine the urgency of announcements. This enhances the system’s ability to highlight critical information. Overall, the model design integrates machine learning techniques with domain-specific rules to achieve accurate and efficient classification of announcements. This contributes significantly to the intelligent behavior of the system.
3.11 Backend and API Development
The backend component of the system serves as the core processing unit responsible for managing data flow, executing business logic, and facilitating communication between system components. It is implemented using a RESTful architecture, which allows different parts of the system to interact through well-defined API endpoints. The backend is developed using a lightweight web framework such as Flask, which supports rapid development and seamless integration with machine learning libraries. The backend handles multiple operations, including receiving input from the admin interface, validating data, storing information in the database, and communicating with the AI model.
When an admin submits an announcement, the data is sent to the backend in JSON format. The backend first performs input validation to ensure that all required fields are present and correctly formatted. This step is crucial for maintaining data integrity and preventing errors during processing. After validation, the announcement is stored in the database, where it is assigned a unique identifier. The backend then forwards the announcement content to the AI model for classification. Once the model returns the category and priority level, the backend updates the database with this information. The system also includes a context-aware filtering mechanism, which is implemented within the backend. This mechanism evaluates factors such as location, time, and audience to determine which announcements should be displayed on specific smart screens.
The backend exposes several API endpoints, including:
•	POST /announcements → Create announcement 
•	GET /announcements → Retrieve announcements 
•	PUT /announcements → Update announcement 
•	DELETE /announcements → Remove announcement 
These endpoints enable seamless communication between the frontend and backend, allowing real-time updates of the system. According to IEEE (2018), RESTful APIs are widely used in modern systems due to their scalability, flexibility, and efficiency. The backend also ensures that data is returned in a structured JSON format, which is easily processed by the frontend. This design enables dynamic content updates on smart screens without requiring manual intervention.

3.12 System Workflow 
The system workflow describes the complete operational process of the proposed system, illustrating how data flows from input to output. It provides a comprehensive understanding of how different system components interact to achieve the desired functionality.
Considering Fig 3.14 workflow begins when the admin submits an announcement through the admin interface. This announcement includes details such as title, description, category, priority, and target audience. The data is transmitted to the backend server in JSON format, ensuring structured communication. Upon receiving the data, the backend performs validation to ensure that the input meets the required criteria. The validated data is then stored in the database for future retrieval and processing. This step ensures that all announcements are properly recorded within the system.
Next, the announcement is passed to the AI classification module, where it is analyzed and assigned a category and priority level. This automated classification process reduces manual effort and improves efficiency. Following classification, the system performs context-aware analysis. This involves evaluating environmental factors such as the location of the smart screen, the time of day, and the intended audience. Based on this analysis, the system filters and selects the most relevant announcements for display. The selected announcements are then ranked based on priority, ensuring that urgent information is displayed prominently. High-priority announcements may also trigger special display features such as full-screen alerts. Finally, the processed data is sent to the smart screen interface, where it is displayed to users. The frontend continuously communicates with the backend to fetch updated data, enabling real-time updates and ensuring that users always have access to the latest information. This workflow demonstrates the integration of data processing, machine learning, and context-aware computing to deliver an intelligent and efficient announcement system.
